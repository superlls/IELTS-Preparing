# Agree or Disagree 论文模板与范文

## 题目
**The most important aim of science should be to improve people's lives. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?**

---

## 📋 核心模板骨架

### 【开头段 Introduction】
**改写题目 + 立场声明**

Some people believe that [改写题目内容]. I generally/partly agree with this idea because [核心观点简述].

*或者*

While science serves multiple purposes, I believe that improving people's lives should remain its primary objective.

---

### 【正文段1 - 核心支持论点】
**论点：应用科学直接改善人民生活水平**

- **主题句**：One strong argument for this idea is that applied science has directly improved human living standards across multiple domains.
  
- **展开 1（因→果）**：
  Since medical science has advanced, it can lead to / contribute to the treatment of diseases and increase life expectancy.
  
- **展开 2（具体群体 + 特点 + 原因）**：
  This is particularly true for people in developing countries, who lack access to basic healthcare, as medical innovations can save millions of lives.
  
- **例子**：
  For example, the development of vaccines like COVID-19 vaccines depends on scientific research, which has directly protected public health globally.

---

### 【正文段2 - 支持论点 2】
**论点：改善生活的科学确保了科学本身的可持续发展**

- **主题句**：Moreover, another strong reason supporting this idea is that when science demonstrates practical value to society, it gains sustainable funding and public support.

- **因果展开**：
  Because governments and industries prioritize research that benefits people's daily lives, they can allocate resources more effectively.
  
- **对比展开**：
  Rather than funding purely theoretical research with unclear benefits, investing in practical science enables broader societal progress.
  
- **例子**：
  For instance, scientists who focus on renewable energy can secure funding from both governments and private companies, which demand sustainable solutions for future energy needs.

---

### 【正文段3 - 让步 + 对立】
**承认反面，但加强正方立场**

- **转折主题句**：However, it is important to note that fundamental research without immediate practical applications also has value.

- **反面观点**：
  Although basic science like theoretical physics may not directly improve people's lives, it can eventually contribute to breakthrough innovations.
  
- **主要对象转折**：
  But/Yet, considering limited resources and pressing global challenges, the priority should be applied science that addresses real-world problems.
  
- **缺陷说明**：
  This disadvantage is often associated with inequality and lack of development.
  
- **例子**：
  For example, while space exploration has scientific merit, directing those resources toward medical research or clean water technology might have more immediate impact on people's wellbeing.

---

### 【结尾段 Conclusion】
**重申立场 + 总结论点**

In conclusion, while science has value in multiple forms, I generally agree that improving people's lives should be the primary objective. Scientific advancement that directly addresses human needs—whether in healthcare, agriculture, or environmental protection—creates both immediate benefits and long-term sustainability for scientific progress itself.

## 📝 范文 1：清晰直接版（7 分水平）

**The most important aim of science should be to improve people's lives. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?**

Science has always played a crucial role in human development. I generally agree that improving people's lives should be the primary aim of scientific research.

First, applied science has directly improved our living standards in many ways. Medical science is the most obvious example. The development of vaccines and antibiotics has saved millions of lives worldwide. Similarly, agricultural science has increased food production and nutrition, helping countries overcome hunger. These advancements show that when science focuses on solving real human problems, it creates immediate and measurable benefits.

Second, science that improves people's lives gains stronger support and funding. Governments and companies are more willing to invest in research that delivers practical results. For example, COVID-19 vaccine research received unprecedented funding because it directly addressed a global crisis. This demonstrates that making human welfare a priority is not only ethical but also practical, as it ensures science can continue and advance.

However, I should acknowledge that fundamental research, such as theoretical physics or astronomy, may not have obvious practical applications. These fields contribute to human knowledge and may eventually lead to important discoveries. But considering our limited resources and urgent global challenges like poverty and disease, we should prioritize science that directly improves people's lives.

In conclusion, while pure research has value, science's main goal should be improving human welfare. This approach benefits both society and the scientific community itself by creating practical solutions and sustainable support for research.

## 📝 范文 2：论证深化版（8 分水平）

**The most important aim of science should be to improve people's lives. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?**

While science serves multiple functions in society, I firmly believe that enhancing human welfare should remain its paramount objective, although this should not entirely dismiss the value of fundamental research.

The most compelling argument for this position lies in the tangible benefits that applied science has delivered across multiple sectors. Medical science exemplifies this principle: the eradication of smallpox and the development of modern treatments have not only extended human lifespan but also improved quality of life for billions. Similarly, agricultural innovations have transformed food production, enabling developing nations to achieve food security. This is particularly crucial for vulnerable populations in low-income countries who lack access to basic services. When scientists focus on renewable energy or water purification technologies, which demand sustainable solutions for future generations, their work directly addresses humanity's most pressing needs.

Furthermore, science that prioritizes human improvement secures more substantial funding and societal support. Because the public tends to value research with visible outcomes that benefit their daily lives, governments allocate budgets more effectively toward applied sciences. Rather than distributing limited resources across purely theoretical fields with unclear benefits, this approach ensures maximum social impact. For instance, pharmaceutical companies that develop life-saving drugs attract far greater investment than abstract mathematical research, thereby accelerating progress in critical areas.

Admittedly, fundamental research in theoretical physics or cosmology, though lacking immediate practical applications, contributes to human knowledge and may eventually spawn unexpected innovations. Nevertheless, given the finite resources available and urgent global challenges—poverty, disease, environmental degradation—strategic prioritization toward applied science becomes not merely desirable but morally imperative. This disadvantage of over-emphasizing abstract research is often associated with widening inequality and stunted development in crucial sectors.

In conclusion, science's predominant aim should unequivocally be improving people's lives. Allocating resources toward addressing real-world problems—from healthcare to environmental sustainability—creates synergistic benefits: immediate human welfare improvements coupled with sustainable scientific advancement.

## 📝 范文 3：高阶论证版（8.5-9 分水平）

**The most important aim of science should be to improve people's lives. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?**

The question of science's fundamental purpose presents a compelling tension between utilitarian application and intellectual curiosity. While I acknowledge the intrinsic value of theoretical inquiry, I contend that optimizing human welfare should constitute science's predominant mandate in resource allocation and institutional priorities.

The empirical case for welfare-oriented science is robust and multifaceted. Applied sciences have catalyzed unprecedented improvements in human longevity, health, and socioeconomic mobility. Immunology and microbiology have virtually eliminated infectious diseases that once devastated populations. Agronomic advances have increased global agricultural yield by over 50% in recent decades, directly correlating with poverty reduction in developing economies. This is particularly significant for marginalized communities in the Global South, whose survival depends on innovations in medicine, sanitation, and sustainable agriculture. When scientists concentrate efforts on renewable energy technologies or drought-resistant crops—fields that directly serve societal infrastructure needs—they simultaneously advance both human flourishing and scientific credibility.

Moreover, a welfare-centric framework creates positive externalities for scientific enterprise itself. Research addressing tangible human problems attracts robust public funding, attracts talented researchers, and maintains public trust in scientific institutions. When populations witness direct improvements in healthcare, nutrition, or living conditions attributable to scientific progress, they more readily support continued investment. Conversely, excessive emphasis on abstract theoretical pursuits, disconnected from practical outcomes, risks eroding public confidence and funding streams. For example, nations that prioritized medical research during the pandemic secured both humanitarian outcomes and enhanced scientific infrastructure.

That said, foundational research in pure mathematics, theoretical physics, or basic biology, lacking immediate applicability, possesses undeniable epistemic value and has occasionally yielded transformative breakthroughs. However, this acknowledgment should not obscure a fundamental reality: given constrained budgets and pressing global exigencies—climate emergency, infectious disease, material poverty—judicious resource allocation demands prioritizing welfare-generating science. This apparent tension between applied and theoretical research is often resolved by directing the bulk of resources toward applied domains while permitting limited foundational inquiry.

In conclusion, while science encompasses multiple worthwhile pursuits, institutional priorities and resource allocation should overwhelmingly favor research that demonstrably improves human wellbeing. This approach achieves both ethical imperatives and practical sustainability: it addresses humanity's most urgent needs while paradoxically strengthening science's institutional foundations and public legitimacy.

## 💡 关键提升技巧

| 要素 | 中等水平 | 高级水平 |
|------|--------|--------|
| **论点** | "疫苗救了很多人" | "疫苗改变了人口结构，促进了发展中国家的经济增长" |
| **例子** | 简单陈述 | 说明"为什么"和"对谁最重要" |
| **让步** | 承认反面存在 | 承认反面，但解释为什么主要观点更强 |
| **词汇** | 基础但清晰 | 精准且多样："catalyzed", "mandate", "intrinsic value" |
| **逻辑链** | 线性：原因→结果 | 立体：原因→结果→长期影响→资源配置 |
